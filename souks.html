13 Most Popular Dubai Souks in Dubai

Souks and markets are a standard in Arabian tradition and shopping culture, and Dubai is no exception to this. Dubai’s souks & markets are home to everything from exotic scents, gold, raw silks and rare fabrics to unique culinary destinations. A part of your Dubai memory needs to involve haggling and bargaining at some of the most bustling souks and markets of the world.

1. Dubai Gold Souk – Best Gold & Jewelry Shopping Destination in Dubai

You’ll be spoiled for choice and your biggest problem will be choosing what to purchase. Dominant local brands compete with 100s of small gold merchants at this world-renowned gold souk. Dubai has developed a reputation for having low gold prices and that’s the cause of this market’s booming global popularity. If you’re in a budget, many shops here also sell gold-plated and gold-filled jewelry. This is a destination for your jewelry needs regardless of your budget. Also Try: Essential Things to See & Do in Dubai (Do NOT Miss Out!)

Location: Deira, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 5/5

2. Dubai Perfume Souk – Best Perfume Shopping Destination in Dubai

Crafting scents has been a feature of Gulf traditions for centuries. The locally developed scents are called oud, and they are natural made from herbs, spices, and secret ingredients exclusive to different merchants. Local perfume brands and traditional scents crowd this souk so you’ll have options for a scent like never before. Expect to be handed 2 to 3 samples every second, but have no worries as all the merchants are polite and extremely respectful; after all, they do want to secure that sale. The best part is that some merchants here will create a custom scent just to match your personality! This souk is an amazing Dubai destination. Also Try: Best FREE Beaches in Dubai (Yes, FREE!)

Location: Deira, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 5/5

3. Dubai Spice Souk – Best Fresh Spice Market in Dubai

One of the oldest souks in not just Dubai, but the entire United Arab Emirates. You’ll find a bustling, vibrant atmosphere, where each shop is colorful and bright due to the fragrant herbs and spices it carries; everything from cardamom, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, vanilla pods and saffron, to shisha tobacco and pipes, stunning traditional lamps, and homewares is available here. Even if spices aren’t your part of your palate, this is a great opportunity to appreciate a massive, authentic souk. Also Try: Ranked – Dubai’s Adventure Zones (Most will FAIL!)

Location: Deira, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 5/5

4. Dubai Textile Souk (also known as the Old Souk) – Best Textile Market in Dubai

You can find every fabric, texture, and textile imaginable here. From yards of raw beautiful silks, handmade embroidery and saris to traditional Arab dress, this market has it all! The Dubai Textile Souk hosts merchants from across the globe selling their prized materials, all clustered in one convenient spot. You are about to be spoiled with choices! Additionally, this souk has maintained its traditional roots so the classical Arabian architecture remains to date, making a trip here a double combo. Also Try: Dubai’s Best Water Sports (Some are SCARY!)

Location: Bur Dubai, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 4.5/5

5. Dubai Souk Madinat Jumeirah – Most Beautiful Souk in Dubai

If you hear or read Jumeirah, expect luxury. This extremely popular souk mixes traditional architecture with modern infrastructure and luxury. The architecture and shop alignment are themed on traditional Arabian designs. You won’t be able to haggle here as the prices are fixed; often, the rates will be quite pricey. This is an experience in itself as location is beautiful and is a small distance from the Burj al Arab. Also Try: Top Water Adventures in Dubai

Location: Jumeirah, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 4.5/5

6. Dubai ARTE – Best Art Souk in Dubai

A market dedicated to local arts and crafts! The only drawbacks is that its available exclusively on the first and second Fridays of every month. This spot features some established local artists and plenty of up-and-coming young talents. This is both a platform that is an opportunity for many to showcase their work, and a platform for many to spectate, observe, and purchase inspiring, beautiful pieces of artwork.

Location: Festival City, Dubai, United Arab Emirates (first Friday); Times Square Center, Dubai, United Arab Emirates (second Friday)
Dubaing Score™: 3.5/5

7. Dubai Souk al Bahar – Newest Souk in Dubai

This is one of Dubai’s modern souks, which blend traditional Arabian architecture with modern infrastructure and luxury; it’s located next to the Burj al Khalifa. Bargains and haggles are not a part of this souk as prices are fixed and will often be quite high as many shops exclusively sell luxury products.

Location: Downtown Dubai, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 3.5/5

8. Dubai Marina Market – Best Trend Market in Dubai

Dubai is fulfilling all forms of demands. This is an “up-to-trend” market dedicated to vintage accessories, hand-made products, fair-trade products, art, and photography. This market is outdoors and most stalls are small and exclusive to a specific genre of products. While it’s a dwarf compared to competing souks, this one is placed on the Marina Walk; you’ll have plenty of amazing dining options and a wonderful option of taking a walk beside a man-made Marina in Dubai. Also Try: 5 Best Promenades in Dubai

Location: Marina, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 3/5

10. Dubai Naif Souk – Leading Bargain Shopping Destination in Dubai

Once a prominent market for camel traders, Naif Souq has developed into a marketplace. You won’t get the traditional feel and vibe you’d receive from other souks in Dubai and the setting has a feel of a warehouse adorned with shops. Nonetheless, this is a popular destination for bargains on watches and leather handbags.

Location: Deira, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 2.5/5

11. Dubai Karama Market – Popular Market for Knockoffs in Dubai

This is not a traditional souk. Rather, it’s an open plaza-style marketplace selling pretty much anything. The prime draw here are the counterfeit branded products sold at many of the shops at dirt-cheap rates.

Location: Karama, Dubai, United Arab Emirates
Dubaing Score™: 2.5/5
