Top 13 Must-See Forts & Palaces in Dubai

It’s easy to get lost with awe at the remarkable infrastructure and skyline Dubai holds today. Elite hotels, a metro, gleaming malls and towers, and a state of the art infrastructure are achievements of Dubai’s recent past. But venturing just a few decades back would bring about the traditional infrastructure of this booming emirate. Here’s the best guide to forts, palaces, and notable houses in Dubai that will allow you to peak into the past and get a grasp of the brilliant heritage and traditional architecture that was once a standard in Dubai.

1. Sheikh Saeed Al Maktoum House Dubai – Best Traditional, Archaic Palace in Dubai

While a museum now, this House used to belong to a late ruler of Dubai. It features impressive traditional architecture and gallery. Ranked: Best Heritage Locations in Dubai (Almost GONE 2017)

Cost: AED 3 for Adults, AED 1 for children
Timings: Saturday to Thursday from 8.30am to 9pm, Friday from 3pm to 8.30pm
Contact: 800-4003
Dubaing Score™: 5/5

2. BONUS: Qasr Al Hosn Abu Dhabi – Most Popular UAE Fort Among Tourists

This is the first constructed building in Abu Dhabi. It has been developed into a fort and showcases a location for the Abu Dhabi throne till 1966. It showcases the history of the city’s rulers. Ranked: Best Things to Do & See in Dubai (STUNNING)

Cost: Free
Timings: Daily from 7.30am to 6.30pm
Contact: 800 724
Dubaing Score™: 5/5

3. BONUS: Al Hisn Fort Museum Sharjah – One of the Oldest Forts in UAE

This fort was built in 1823 and today serves as tool to teach about Sharjah’s royal family, how its people have lived for the past 200 years, and a look into classical architecture. The museum features archaic photos and artifacts cored on Sharjah’s heritage. It still holds on to the genuine cannons that were built at the time of the Fort’s construction. Also Try: 10 FREE Time Outs in Dubai (Yes, FREE!)

Cost: AED 10 per Adult, AED 5 per child
Timings: Saturday to Thursday from 8am to 8pm, Friday from 4pm to 8pm
Contact: 06 5685500
Dubaing Score™: 4.5/5

4. BONUS: Bait Al Naboodah – Oldest Merchant Fort in Dubai

This is a house that belonged to a prominent pearl merchant. Touring the now-museum house allows you to see the principles and customs of the country’s ancestors. The house is made of coral and gives a view of how Emirati houses looked during the 1800s. Also Try: Ranked – Best Souks in Dubai (Only 2017!)

Cost: AED 10 per Adult, AED 5 per child
Timings: Saturday to Thursday from 8am to 8pm; Friday from 4pm to 8pm
Contact: (971) 06 556-6000
Dubaing Score™: 4/5

5. BONUS: Umm ul Quwain Fort – Oldest Fort in UAE

This fort dates back to 1768! The fort is now a museum featuring valuable pieces from the Ed-dour historical site and the pieces’ history goes back to the Roman Empire.

Cost: AED 4 per person, free for children under 15
Timings: Saturday to Thursday from 8am to 1pm and 5pm to 8pm, Friday from 5pm to 8pm
Contact: 06 7650888

6. BONUS: Bait Shaikh Saeed Bin Hamad Al Qasimi in Sharjah – Popular Traditional, Archaic Palace

This house belonged to one of the country’s royals and is a spectacular display of the local architecture and heritage. Its artifacts include valuable pieces from Islamic history.

Cost: AED 5 per Adult, free for children
Timings: Saturday to Thursday from 8am to 8pm, Friday from 4pm to 8pm

While not clear from the name, this museum is essentially a fort. The museum features valuable artefacts discovered across the emirate and give a valuable insight into the country’s traditional architecture and heritage. Ranked: Best Science Museums in Dubai

8. Al Oqaili Museum Dubai – Archaic House of Country’s Most Revered Poet

This house used to belong to one of the country’s most celebrated poets. The house has been maintained and turned into a museum featuring Oqaili’s poetry and a view into his life. Ranked: Best Art Museums in Dubai

9. BONUS: Al Manama Fort Ajman – Best UAE Fort for Viewing Artifacts

This fort-turned-museum is located in Ajman and houses a large collection of archaeological artefacts, manuscripts, and old weapons. This is the only museum in Ajman.

Cost: AED 5 per person
Timings: Saturday to Thursday from 8am to 8pm
