Dubai Musts: Dhows & Safaris

There are certain must-dos for each tourist destination. For Dubai, the safaris and dhows are a necessity.

The safaris are a trip of a life-time, extremely enjoyable yet extremely affordable. Get ready for a fast and thrilling experience through the ups and downs of the desert dunes! These will follow up by optional 4x4 or BMX rides through the desert, camel rides, and a night full of events ranging from colorful and vibrant traditional arab dances to jaw-dropping belly dances. Of course, there’ll also be delicious BBQ meals for the guests; vegetarian diets are also supported.

Most safari operators will request you be picked up from a specific rendezvous, and a few will offer VIP packages which will involve hotel a pick-up. The safari operators work along with a desert camp operator to build an amazing experience. Dubai desert safaris take up 8 hours with all the amazing things they include. Avoid branding frills and focus on what extra features your money buys. Upon launch, we will offer deals for unbeatable rates on safaris.

The dhows are large traditional Arab boats: subtly lit, wooden, and hosting many activities. Unlike the thrills of a safari, these offer a more calm and relaxing environment as you drift through waters flowing through Dubai, capturing the best skyline in the world. Dhow trips are like a mini cruise and will last for a few hours, allowing you to enjoy a scrumptious meal, accommodating all sorts of diets. Dhow trips can take between 2 to 4 hours. City Views from the Sky

The most spectacular views of Dubai would need you to get your feet off the ground. Conveniently, the city offers many ways of getting your eyes high up in the sky.

Burj Khalifa’s At The Top is the most common and affordable way of getting a sky-high view of the city. It sets you 456m above the ground on the 124th & 125th floors of the tallest building in the world. Infants enter free (age 4 or less), but adults are allowed inside for a base rate of AED 125 between 8.30 AM to midnight; the only exception is 5.30 PM to 7 PM (sunset views would cost AED 200).

Burj Khalifa’s At the Top Sky sets you at the building’s 148th level. This will cost AED 350 during normal hours and AED 500 during sunset hours. This is not recommended because the extra sum does not offer you a much better view. The difference is marginal because the views from the 124th & 125th floors already give you a sight reaching to the depths of Dubai.
